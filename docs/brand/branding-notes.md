# Karavyn — Brand Identity Notes

> **Version:** 1.0
> **Last updated:** March 2026

---

## The Name: Karavyn

**Karavyn** (kah-RAH-vin) is derived from "caravan" — a group of people traveling together. The modified spelling makes it ownable, distinctive, and domain/handle-friendly while retaining the core association with group movement and togetherness.

### Why It Works

- **Movement + togetherness in one word.** A caravan is inherently a group on a journey. This captures both the convoy use case (literally) and the broader "people moving through the world together" concept.
- **Cross-activity resonance.** "Caravan" isn't limited to cars — it evokes road trips, group travel, festival arrivals, and any collective movement toward a shared destination.
- **Phonetically memorable.** Three syllables, rhythmic stress on the second syllable, easy to say in conversation ("let me start a Karavyn").
- **Distinctive spelling.** The "-vyn" ending avoids exact trademark overlap with "caravan" and creates a unique visual identity. It reads modern without being contrived.
- **Mode-compatible.** "Karavyn Drive," "Karavyn Hang," "Karavyn Walk" all work as mode labels.

### Potential Risks

- Pronunciation ambiguity on first encounter (is it "ka-RAV-in" or "KAIR-a-vin"?) — mitigated by repeated exposure and audio branding.
- The "caravan" association may initially skew toward travel/driving — mitigated by broader brand positioning and visual identity.

## Brand Positioning

### Tagline Candidates

| Tagline | Tone | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **"Move together."** | Clean, universal, action-oriented | Primary tagline — works across all use cases |
| **"Your group, live."** | Direct, product-descriptive | App store subtitle, onboarding |
| **"Stay in sync. Stay together."** | Warm, reassuring | Marketing, social |
| **"One session. Everyone's in."** | Inclusive, low-friction | Growth/virality messaging |
| **"The group coordination app."** | Functional, clear | App store title/description |

**Recommended primary tagline:** **"Move together."**

It's short, universal (works for driving, walking, partying, traveling), implies both physical movement and social coordination, and avoids being overly specific to one use case.

## Brand Tone

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | What It Means | What It's Not |
|---|---|---|
| **Energetic** | Karavyn is about people doing things together in the real world. The tone should feel alive, kinetic. | Not hyper or exhausting. No all-caps enthusiasm. |
| **Inclusive** | Anyone with a group and a plan can use Karavyn. No gatekeeping. | Not generic. The brand has a point of view — it's about real-world coordination, not everything. |
| **Trustworthy** | Location sharing is sensitive. The brand must feel reliable and respectful. | Not corporate or legal-sounding. Trust through transparency, not formality. |
| **Real-world** | Karavyn exists for physical-world experiences. The digital layer serves the real one. | Not a social network, not a content platform, not a virtual hangout. |
| **Direct** | Say what needs to be said. No filler, no buzzwords. | Not blunt or cold. Warm directness. |

### Voice Examples

- **Good:** "Start a session. Everyone follows along."
- **Good:** "Your group, on one map. No more 'where are you?' texts."
- **Bad:** "Revolutionizing how groups experience the world together through cutting-edge real-time technology."
- **Bad:** "OMG link up with your squad and never lose them again!!! "

### Audience-Appropriate Register

The primary audience (18-30, social, mobile-native) responds to:
- Clarity over cleverness
- Confidence over hype
- Utility messaging ("this solves your problem") over aspiration messaging ("imagine a world where...")
- Casual but not juvenile

## Visual Direction (Guidance)

### Color Territory

- **Primary:** Warm, saturated tone — think amber, coral, or warm orange. Evokes energy, movement, warmth of gathering.
- **Secondary:** Deep contrast — dark charcoal or navy for UI backgrounds, map overlays.
- **Accent:** Bright signal colors for real-time indicators (green for "here," blue for "on the way," red for "need help").
- **Avoid:** Cold blues (too corporate), pastels (too soft for a movement/coordination app), neon (too aggressive).

### Typography Territory

- **Display / Brand:** A distinctive sans-serif with personality — geometric or rounded, not a default system font. Should feel modern and friendly.
- **UI / Body:** Clean, highly readable sans-serif optimized for mobile. Legibility on map overlays is a hard constraint.

### Iconography / Motifs

- **Movement lines / paths** — suggest travel, routes, flow
- **Group dots / markers** — abstract representation of people on a map
- **Connection arcs** — lines between points suggesting coordination
- **Avoid:** Literal car imagery (limits the brand to driving), social media iconography (likes, hearts, follows), corporate meeting imagery

### App Icon Direction

The app icon should work at 1024px and 29px. Recommendations:
- Abstract mark based on converging paths or a group of dots moving in formation
- Use the primary brand color as the icon background
- No text in the icon (doesn't scale)
- Should feel alive, not static — implied motion

## Naming Conventions Within the Product

### Session Language

| Concept | Term | Not |
|---|---|---|
| A coordinated group activity | **Session** | Event, room, trip, convoy |
| Starting a session | **Start a session** | Create an event, host a convoy |
| Joining a session | **Join** | RSVP, attend, enter |
| The shareable link | **Invite link** | Session link, meeting link |
| Ending a session | **End session** | Close, terminate, stop |

**"Session" is the core noun.** It's neutral enough to cover driving, partying, meeting, and traveling. It's familiar from tech contexts (Zoom sessions, gaming sessions) without being tied to any single activity.

### Mode Language

| Mode | Label | Subtitle |
|---|---|---|
| Driving together | **Drive** | "Convoy mode" (optional descriptor) |
| Walking / moving on foot | **Walk** | "Moving together on foot" |
| Stationary gathering | **Hang** | "Gathering at a spot" |

### Status Language

| Status | Display Text | Context |
|---|---|---|
| On the way | **On My Way** | Member is heading to the session location or group |
| Arrived | **Here** | Member has arrived |
| Delayed | **Running Late** | Member is behind schedule |

### Ping Language

| Ping | Display Text | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Regroup | **Regroup** | "Everyone come back together" |
| Moving | **Moving** | "The group is moving to a new spot" |
| Need help | **Need Help** | "I need assistance" |
| Stopping | **Stopping** | "I'm pulling over / taking a break" (Drive mode) |
